Why is mental health important for students?

Research shows that high levels of mental health are associated with increased learning, creativity and productivity, more pro-social behaviour and positive social relationships, and with improved physical health and life expectancy.


Why do students face mental health issues?

Peer pressure is a type of risk to mental health in teens. Up to 70% of teens and young adults state that they feel anxiety and depression are an issue in their community. Over half of all high schoolers believe bullying continues to be an issue. Fifty percent of high schoolers also believe drug addiction affects their peers and 45% feel drinking alcohol is an issue. In other communities, poverty, gangs, and teen pregnancy are major concerns
